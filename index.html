ESCAPE: A Standardized Benchmark for Multilabel Antimicrobial Peptide Classification

Sebastian Ojeda, Rafael Velasquez, Nicolás Aparicio, Juanita Puentes, Paula Cárdenas, Nicolás Andrade, Gabriel González, Sergio Rincón, Carolina Muñoz-Camargo, and Pablo Arbeláez
Universidad de Los Andes, Colombia
The Thirty-ninth Annual Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS 2025)

ESCAPE provides a unified dataset, tasks, and evaluation for multilabel AMP classification.

Expanded Standardized Collection for Antimicrobial Peptide Evaluation (ESCAPE) is an experimental framework for multilabel antimicrobial peptide classification. It combines a curated dataset, a benchmark, and a transformer-based baseline integrating both sequence and structural information.

Abstract

Antimicrobial peptides have emerged as promising molecules to combat antimicrobial resistance. However, fragmented datasets and inconsistent annotations hinder computational approaches. We present the ESCAPE framework integrating over 80 000 peptides from 27 validated repositories. It separates AMPs from negatives and organizes activities across antibacterial, antifungal, antiviral, and antiparasitic classes. Our transformer-based model leveraging sequence and structure achieves a 2.56 % improvement in mean AP over prior methods. ESCAPE provides a comprehensive, reproducible evaluation platform for AI-driven peptide discovery. The dataset is available here and the baseline code here.

ESCAPE Database

The ESCAPE Dataset compiles over 80 000 sequences from 27 public repositories, addressing data fragmentation and inconsistent annotation. It separates AMPs from non-AMPs and structures functional labels hierarchically across four activity types.

ESCAPE integrates peptides from 27 curated databases covering antibacterial, antifungal, antiviral, and antiparasitic functions, capturing diverse mechanisms such as membrane disruption and inhibition of cell-wall biosynthesis. Non-AMP sequences were selected from UniProt and curated negatives following the TransImbAMP filtering approach, excluding entries with antimicrobial-related keywords to ensure a high-confidence contrast between positive and negative sets.

The ESCAPE dataset is available on Harvard Dataverse.

ESCAPE Baseline

The ESCAPE Baseline is a dual-branch transformer using sequence and structural information. Amino-acid sequences are processed through one encoder, while peptide distance matrices feed another; bidirectional cross-attention fuses modalities, yielding state-of-the-art multilabel performance.
